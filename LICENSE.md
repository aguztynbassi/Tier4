The GNU Free Documentation License (GFDL) is a license specifically designed for software documentation and other creative works. Here is a summary of its main points:

1. **Distribution and Copying:** The GFDL allows for the distribution and copying of the document, either in its original or modified form, under certain conditions.
2. **Modifications:** Users can modify the document, but they must include a copy of the license with the modified version and provide a clear notice of the changes made.
3. **Compatibility:** The GFDL is compatible with other GNU licenses, allowing documents licensed under the GFDL to be combined with other works using GNU free software licenses.
4. **Later Versions:** Copyright holders can allow the document to be distributed under later versions of the GFDL.
5. **Invariability:** The GFDL allows for the inclusion of invariant sections, which are parts of the document that cannot be modified or removed in modified versions of the work.
6. **Front and Back Cover:** Documents licensed under the GFDL must include a front and back cover with certain information about the license and the author.
7. **Printed Copies:** If printed copies of the document are distributed, they must also include a copy of the license.
8. **Commercial Use:** The GFDL allows for commercial use of the document but imposes certain restrictions and obligations regarding attribution and distribution.
